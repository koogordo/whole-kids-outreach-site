Missouri Resident Tax Credits

Whole Kids Outreach is often awarded tax credits from the State of Missouri, which can be offered directly to qualified donors who pay taxes in the State of Missouri. These credits are based on a nonprofit organization’s quality and fiscal responsibility, as well as the measurable outcomes of the project presented.

Currently, YOP and NAP tax credits are available through Whole Kids Outreach.

[WKO regularly applies for tax credit program grants and the availability will change as projects are approved and grants are expended. Changes will be noted here, as necessary.]

Further Information:

  • The Missouri Tax Credit Forms website provides both individual and business forms required for the credits.
  • The two basic types of programs through which WKO can typically offer tax credits to donors are the Youth Opportunities Program (YOP) and the Neighborhood Assistance Program (NAP).

Both Programs:

  • A donation qualifies for tax credits in the year it is made. If the amount of credit exceeds state tax liability for the taxable year in which the donation is made, the credits can be carried over for the next five successive years.
  • Donations may be in the form of cash or appreciated securities.
  • If donating appreciated securities, the donor receives the tax credit based on the mean market value of the securities on the date the transfer is authorized. In addition, a donor making a gift of stock will owe no capital gains tax on any appreciation.
  • Donors are also able to claim the tax-deduction for their gifts to WKO.

Youth Opportunity Program (YOP)

  • A donor filing an itemized return in Missouri can claim 50% of the donation as a state tax credit to the limit of his/her taxable income.
  • A minimum gift of $200 to WKO is required to obtain these credits.

Neighborhood Assistance Program (NAP)

  • A qualified donor can claim 70% of the donation as a state tax credit to the limit of his/her taxable income.
  • An eligible NAP donor is one who has business income in Missouri and be one of the following: a corporation; a small business corporation (S-Corporation); a limited liability corporation/partnership; a partnership; an individual partner in a partnership, member of a limited liability corporation or shareholder in an S-Corporation; a bank, credit institution, savings and loan association, credit union, farmer’s cooperative credit association or building and loan; an insurance company; an individual who has rental property in Missouri, has royalty income in Missouri, operates a sole proprietorship in Missouri, and/or operates a farm in Missouri.
  • A minimum gift of $10,000 to WKO is required to obtain these credits. In addition, donors who have not experienced WKO are asked to visit the Center in Ellington prior to making a gift.
