WKO History

Whole Kids Outreach was created in 1999 as the result of a yearlong reorganization and restructuring process of its parent organization, Whole Health Outreach.


Milestones


1989 - Parent Organization

Whole Health Outreach (WHO) opens its doors to provide a local, preventive and personal response to the rural health crisis in southeast Missouri.

1997 - Unmet Need

With Saint Louis University School of Public Health, staff and board of WHO embark on a year-long study of the needs of birth to three-year-old population in service area. Results of this study indicate it would be beneficial to create a new organization specifically targeting the health and well-being of women, children and families.

Late 1998 - Location Set

A grant obtained from the Daughters of Charity Foundation makes it possible to purchase a large house set on 10 acres in Ellington, Missouri that can serve as the Whole Kids Outreach Center.

January 1, 1999

Whole Kids Outreach is incorporated as a nonprofit service organization.
